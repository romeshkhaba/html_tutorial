HTML ATTRIBUTES AND ENTITIES

Attributes give extra information to HTML elements. They are written inside the opening tag and usually come as a name and value pair such as class="card" or id="hero".

Common global attributes include class, id, title, style, lang, dir, hidden, and custom data-* attributes.

Entities are special codes used to display reserved characters or symbols in HTML. For example, &lt; shows the less-than symbol, &amp; shows an ampersand, and &nbsp; creates a non-breaking space.

This paragraph shows how attributes can add identity, styling hooks, and extra meaning to an element.

These entities are useful in HTML: < for less-than, > for greater-than, & for ampersand, and   for a fixed space.
